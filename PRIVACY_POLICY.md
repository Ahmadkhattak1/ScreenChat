# ScreenChat Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 14, 2026

ScreenChat is a browser extension that helps you ask questions about the page you are viewing. This policy explains what data ScreenChat collects, how it is used, and who it is shared with.

## Data ScreenChat Collects

- Google account information you use to sign in, such as your name, email address, profile photo, and authentication tokens.
- The current page URL and visible page content that ScreenChat uses to answer your prompt.
- An optional screenshot of the current page when you enable the "Attach Screen" feature.
- Chat prompts, assistant replies, saved conversation history, and profile details you choose to store in ScreenChat.
- Basic operational data needed to keep the service working, such as request timestamps and diagnostics for abuse prevention and troubleshooting.

## How ScreenChat Uses Data

- To authenticate your account and keep you signed in.
- To answer the questions you ask about the page you are viewing.
- To save your chat history and profile so you can restore previous sessions.
- To secure the service, investigate abuse, and maintain reliability.
- To comply with legal obligations.

## How ScreenChat Shares Data

ScreenChat only shares data with service providers needed to operate the product:

- Google Firebase, for authentication and stored account data.
- The AI provider configured for the backend, currently OpenAI or Replicate, to generate responses.
- The hosting provider that runs the ScreenChat backend.

ScreenChat does not sell user data. ScreenChat does not use user data for personalized advertising. ScreenChat does not transfer or sell user data to data brokers or information resellers.

## Your Controls

- You can choose whether to include a screenshot with a message by toggling "Attach Screen" on or off.
- You can sign out of ScreenChat at any time.
- You can request deletion of your stored ScreenChat data using the instructions in `DATA_DELETION.md`.

## Security

ScreenChat sends extension-to-backend traffic over HTTPS in production. Authentication information and stored data are handled using modern security controls provided by the backend and its service providers.

## Limited Use Statement

The use of information received from Google APIs will adhere to the Chrome Web Store User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.
